I read both files. These are blind A/B results comparing the old vs new `narrative_system.txt` prompt on Mistral-7B, 3 samples per scenario. I judged before checking the answer key; here's the verdict.

**exploration_atmosphere** (A = OLD, B = NEW)

NEW wins 2 of 3. Sample 1: NEW gives concrete spatial layout and plants a discoverable object (the leather book) — better discovery pacing — but truncates mid-word ("It'"). **Cross-cutting issues**

Four of six exploration responses and one quiet_moment response truncate mid-sentence — either raise max tokens or have the prompt push for shorter, complete beats; the latter fits the genre better. Both prompts inherit 7B-Q4 artifacts (hallucinated names, garbled phrases), and with n=3 per scenario one bad sample swings the read — variance is high enough that I'd treat this as directional, not conclusive.

**Bottom line:** NEW is a modest improvement — more concrete scene-building, better discovery hooks, less acting-for-the-player — but it hasn't fixed the restraint failure mode in low-stakes scenes (quiet_moment sample 3). If you iterate again, I'd target two things: an explicit "end on a complete sentence/beat" instruction, and a stronger guard against escalating stakes when the player is just resting.
